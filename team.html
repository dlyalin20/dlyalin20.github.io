MEET THE TEAM

Sarah Cheyney

Co-President

I'm Sarah Cheyney, junior at Stuyvesant High School. I've been a member of Ethics Bowl since my freshman year and previously held the position of Youth Outreach Director, and am now Co-President. I was drawn to Ethics Bowl because of its open, non-judgemental space to discuss all forms of thought and gain new perspectives. After learning about and discussing numerous forms of thought, ethics changed the way I view life and our world (I can pretty much guarantee it'll do the same for you). It also gave me the tools I needed to verbalise my viewpoint powerfully and concisely in all parts of my life, academic and personal. Aside from being a philosophy and debate fanatic, I love creative writing and studying Spanish -- the language and the culture!

Michelle Zhang

Co-President

When I joined this club in freshman year, it was a small team focused on competing at High School Ethics Bowl events, hence the name of the club. I've always enjoyed employing the art of persuasion in writing, however ask me to confidently articulate those same points, and I was not able to. After pushing myself through some difficult-- and embarrassing-- speeches, thanks to the welcoming environment, I found myself thinking more critically, and picking up more skills on smooth, and confident delivery. I came into this team to improve my rhetoric, but I stayed for the rich discussions we had every meeting. The more I was forced to justify every simple assumption, the less black and white everything became. The more I listened and cooperated in the discussion, the more compassion I saw in others with dissenting viewpoints. With our current heated political climate, I found it imperative to preserve the philosophy, nuanced thinking and more importantly, the spirit of Ethics Bowl. Although the competition requires a small team, I'm dedicated to expanding it into a large club with other activities to get more students exposed to ethical studies.
